1. PROJECT IDENTITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Company:        Data Intelligence Lab
Product:        Semantic Data Layer (SDL)
Tagline:        Deterministic structure between noisy raw data
                and language models.
Audience:       a16z Speedrun partners and investment screeners.
                Reading pattern: 60–90 seconds per deck on first pass,
                then deeper if interested.
Format:         Five slides, 16:9, dark theme.
Intended use:   Cold-submission application + warm follow-up meetings.
Tone:           Confident, declarative, low-decoration.
                Closer to a technical whitepaper cover than a marketing
                deck. No stock illustrations, no abstract gradients,
                no AI-generated imagery.


2. STRATEGIC INTENT (read before designing)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is a Deep Tech / Infrastructure deck. The reader is technical and
arrives skeptical — most submissions in this category are GPT-wrappers
with a market-sizing slide attached. The deck must signal architectural
seriousness within ten seconds of opening slide 1.

Three signals the design must carry across every slide:

  - Substance over decoration. No element exists for visual effect alone.
  - Mathematical precision. Whitespace, alignment, and typographic rhythm
    matter more than imagery.
  - Quiet confidence. The deck does not perform urgency, scarcity, or
    enthusiasm. It states position.

Every slide should be readable as a printed document. If the deck would
not survive being printed in black-and-white and circulated as a memo,
it has decorated itself wrong.

This deck does not contain "Why Now" or "Why Us" slides. The architecture
itself answers both questions: if the problem on slide 2 is real, "Why Now"
is self-evident; if the solution on slide 3 holds, "Why Us" follows from
the team that built it. The substrate line in the footer carries the
credibility signal. Do not add a Why Now / Why Us slide back during
design iteration.


3. VISUAL LANGUAGE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Reference: the original "What we do next" slide (attached separately as
PNG) establishes the visual language. Use it as the baseline. Improvements
welcome where they reinforce the strategic intent above; deviations away
from this language require discussion.

3.1 Palette
    Background:         Near-black (#0A0A0B or similar). Not pure black.
    Primary text:       Off-white (#E8E8E8 or similar). Not pure white.
    Accent:             Warm yellow / amber (#E8B84A or similar).
                        Used sparingly — keywords in titles, MOVE labels,
                        accent square in headers. Never for body text.
    Muted text:         Mid-gray (#6E6E72 or similar) for metadata,
                        footers, secondary annotations.
    No additional colors. No gradients. No shadows.

3.2 Typography
    Display / h1:       Sans-serif, slightly thin to regular weight.
                        Large (60–80pt on 16:9). Used once per slide
                        for the primary title.
                        Suggested family: Inter, Söhne, or similar
                        modern grotesque. Avoid Helvetica Neue.
                        Avoid serif at display size.
    Body:               Same family as display, regular weight, 18–22pt.
                        Generous line height (1.45–1.55).
    Metadata:           Monospace, uppercase, letter-spaced.
                        Used for headers, footers, MOVE labels.
                        Suggested family: JetBrains Mono, IBM Plex Mono,
                        or similar.
    Quotes / italics:   Italic serif for the IMPLICATION line on slide 5.
                        Optional. Only place where serif appears.

3.3 Accent treatment
    The yellow accent is used to highlight the operative word in titles
    (e.g. "What we do next" — "do next" in yellow). This is a consistent
    device: one or two keywords per title highlighted in the accent color,
    the rest in primary off-white.
    Do not highlight more than 2–3 words per title.
    Do not highlight body text.

3.4 Layout grid
    Slide frame:        Margins generous — minimum 5% on all sides.
    Header band:        Top row contains slide title (left) and
                        slide number / section label (right),
                        both in monospace metadata style.
                        Optional accent square (■ or ▪) to the left
                        of the title.
    Footer band:        Bottom row contains operating-info line (left)
                        and company / contact / pagination (right),
                        both in monospace metadata style.
                        A thin horizontal rule separates the footer
                        from the slide body.
    Body:               Single-column, two-column, or three-column
                        depending on slide. Columns separated by
                        thin vertical rules in muted gray when used.

3.5 Iconography and imagery
    None. No icons, no illustrations, no diagrams unless explicitly
    specified per slide. No photographs. No backgrounds beyond the
    flat dark fill.

5. PER-SLIDE CONTENT (final, do not edit text without sign-off)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 SLIDE 1 — TITLE

  Header (left, monospace, muted):
    SEMANTIC DATA LAYER (SDL)

  Header (right, monospace, muted):
    SPEEDRUN · 2026

  Primary title (display, large, centered or upper-left aligned):
    Deterministic structure between
    noisy raw data and language models.

  Subtitle (body, below title, two short lines):
    LLMs try to reason over an unstructured world.
    SDL gives them structure to reason over.

  Footer (right, monospace, muted):
    BY DATA INTELLIGENCE LAB

  Design notes:
    - This is the highest-stakes slide. Reader spends 5–10 seconds here.
    - Title takes ~60% of vertical space. Generous breathing room above
      and below the subtitle.
    - No accent yellow on title text — the title is the product name in
      action, all primary color. Optional: accent square before the title.


5.2 SLIDE 2 — THE PROBLEM

  Header (left, monospace, muted, with accent square):
    ▪ THE PROBLEM

  Header (right, monospace, muted):
    02 — DIAGNOSIS

  Primary title (display, accent on "Problem"):
    LLMs don't reason over numbers.
    They hide it well.

  Body (three bullets, arrow markers in accent yellow):
    →  9.11 and 9.8 are token sequences, not numbers.
       Models learn their order statistically.

    →  Irrelevant context before a numerical prompt raises
       error rates 300%+ on frontier reasoning models.
       (CatAttack, 2025)

    →  The industry's answer: wrap the LLM around external
       tools. The model still decides when to call them.
       The failure mode stays.

  Closing line (body, larger weight, separated from bullets by whitespace):
    Bigger models hide it better. They don't fix it.

  Footer (left): Operating company · Bootstrapped eight years
  Footer (right): DATA INTELLIGENCE LAB · 02 / 05


5.3 SLIDE 3 — THE SOLUTION

  Header (left, monospace, muted):
    ▪ THE SOLUTION

  Header (right, monospace, muted):
    03 — ARCHITECTURE

  Primary title (display, accent on "Solution"):
    Rules first. Learning second. Language last.

  Subtitle (body):
    We built an analytical pipeline that turns noisy raw
    data into a clean semantic layer for LLMs.

  Body (five-step pipeline, two columns visual: step name | description):
    →  Signal extraction       Pull signal from noise.
    →  Pattern recognition     Identify known patterns
                               (Pattern Genome).
    →  Pattern discovery       Surface new ones
                               (Data-Driven Expansion).
    →  State modelling         Simulate stable states.
    →  Transition analysis     Map hidden states across
                               regime transitions.

  Closing line (body, larger weight, separated from pipeline by whitespace):
    The LLM never generates facts. It receives them.

  Design notes:
    - The five-step pipeline reads as one continuous flow, not five
      independent items. Optional: thin connecting line down the
      left side of the arrows.
    - The closing line is the architectural commitment of the entire
      product. Give it visual weight comparable to the title.


5.4 SLIDE 4 — UNIVERSALITY

  Header (left, monospace, muted):
    ▪ WHY THIS GENERALIZES

  Header (right, monospace, muted):
    04 — UNIVERSALITY

  Primary title (display, accent on "generalizes"):
    Every decision domain has the same structure.

  Body, upper half (five primitives, two-column: label | gloss):
    Environment    lives by its own laws
    Subject        takes actions, receives feedback
    Object         reacts, partially, with noise
    Hidden state   drives outcomes, not directly observable
    Transitions    between regimes change what signals mean

  Body, middle (short paragraph, separated by whitespace):
    Pattern Genome decomposes state into discrete units.
    Data-Driven Expansion discovers new ones.
    The semantic layer carries into any domain
    where signals project from hidden state.

  Body, lower half (four domain lines, arrow markers):
    →  Financial markets     regime, volatility, liquidity
    →  B2B sales             buyer state, deal momentum
    →  Cognitive ops         belief trajectory, attention
    →  Prediction markets    consensus state, information flow

  Closing line:
    SDL was earned in finance, where the cost of error
    is fastest. The same primitives carry forward.

  Design notes:
    - This is the densest slide. Use whitespace aggressively to
      separate the three blocks (primitives / paragraph / domains).
    - The primitives could optionally be rendered as a small
      structured visual (5 boxes or rows) rather than text columns,
      if the designer finds a treatment that reads cleanly in
      under 10 seconds. Default to text.

6. WHAT TO AVOID
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  -  Stock photography, AI-generated imagery, abstract gradients,
     particle effects, "AI brain" visuals, network graph illustrations,
     glowing nodes. The deck is about a deterministic system; visual
     metaphors of fuzzy neural networks contradict the message.

  -  Color beyond the three-color palette (background, off-white text,
     accent yellow). No blue, no green, no red. Muted gray for
     metadata only.

  -  Cluttered slides. If a slide has more than ~80 words of body text,
     consider whether typography hierarchy is doing its job. Whitespace
     is the most important visual element after typography.

  -  Decorative dividers, ornamental rules, page numbers in display
     typography. Metadata stays in monospace, muted, small.

  -  Centered body text. Body text is left-aligned. Titles may be
     left-aligned or centered depending on slide. Default: left-aligned.

  -  Logos in the footer larger than the metadata text. Company name
     in the footer should read as metadata, not as branding.

  -  Animation on transitions in the static export. The deck must read
     cleanly as PDF first; any animation is for live presentation only.

  -  Adding a "Why Now" or "Why Us" slide back. The five-slide
     structure is deliberate. The architecture itself carries
     both arguments.

  -  Adding a closing quote or team voice line after IMPLICATION
     on slide 5. The deck ends on IMPLICATION. Deliberate restraint.
